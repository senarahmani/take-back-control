When the image is fabricated

It's fake. The harm is real — and so is the law that covers it.

A fabricated or AI-generated sexual image of you is a violation even though it never happened. You may feel a pull to prove it isn't real or to find who made it — but the fastest protection isn't proving or chasing. It's removing, documenting, and handing the "who did this" question to people with the authority to answer it.

The checklist and log below stay on your device. Nothing here is uploaded or sent anywhere.
The law is on your side here, explicitly. The federal Take It Down Act covers AI-generated "digital forgeries" of real people, not just real photos. Knowingly sharing a fabricated sexual image of an identifiable person is a crime, and covered platforms must remove it within 48 hours of a valid request. You are not in a legal gray zone.
Two things first

The mindset that protects you

01 Preserve it before it's removed

Counterintuitive but important: screenshot and save the image, the URL, the username, and the date before you get it taken down. You're not keeping it to look at — you're keeping the evidence investigators and platforms need. Store it somewhere only you can reach, then start removals.

02 Don't try to trace who made it

The urge to hunt down the creator is natural — but amateur "tracing" can break the law, destroy admissible evidence, or expose you to claims. Attribution is a job for police or a lawyer, who have subpoena power you don't. Your role is to preserve and report. Hand the "who" to the people authorized to find out.

The plan, in order

Getting it down

Removal of fabricated images leans on platform reports and search removal — both have specific paths for fake/AI content.

1

Report it to the platform — as fabricated

You or a helper~15 min

Report the post/account and say clearly it's a fabricated / AI-generated image distributed without consent. Major platforms have a deepfake/non-consensual path and must act within 48 hours under the Take It Down Act. Keep the confirmation. Notice template below.

2

Remove it from search — fake-content path

Needs the person depicted~30 minHighest impact

Google's removal flow has a specific option for fake sexual content that uses your likeness — you don't have to claim it's real. Use that. Then Bing (which also covers Yahoo and DuckDuckGo), and Yandex/Brave. Deindexing is what stops people from finding it.

3

Consider a police report

Your choice, fullycriminal option

Creating and sharing a sexual deepfake of a real person is criminal under the Take It Down Act and many state laws. If you want to pursue it, bring your preserved evidence to police, and let them do the tracing. CCRI can help you decide whether and how. Reporting is your call — entirely.

4

Report stubborn sites' infrastructure

A helper can do this~10 min per site

For a site that won't comply, report the companies underneath it — CDN (often Cloudflare), host, registrar — citing a non-consensual AI forgery and the Take It Down Act.

    Honest answers

    Questions people ask

    It's fake — can I really get it removed?›

    Yes. You don't have to prove anything is real. Platforms and Google have specific removal paths for fabricated/AI sexual content using your likeness, and the Take It Down Act explicitly covers digital forgeries. "It's fake" is the basis for removal, not an obstacle to it.

    Can you help me find who made it?›

    This guide deliberately won't, and you shouldn't try to either — not because the question doesn't matter, but because amateur tracing can be illegal, can wreck a future case, and can put you at risk. The right move is to preserve the evidence and bring it to police or a lawyer, who can compel the information lawfully.

    The image is on a site overseas. Now what?›

    Focus on making it unfindable rather than deleted at the source: remove it from search, and report the US/EU infrastructure underneath the site (CDN, host, registrar). The CCRI hotline is very good at this.
