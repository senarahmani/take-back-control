You don't have to have the words yet

Whatever happened, you deserve support.

You don't need to know what to call it, whether it "counts," or what you want to do about it. None of that has to be figured out before you reach out. The people below are free, confidential, and trained for exactly this — including the part where you're not sure.

If you're in immediate danger or thinking about harming yourself, please reach out right now. Call your local emergency number (911 in the US). In the US, you can also call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), any time, for any kind of crisis. You don't have to be sure it's "bad enough" to call.
A few true things

Before anything else

It's okay not to know what it was

Uncertainty is one of the most common experiences after something upsetting happens — not a sign that it wasn't real or didn't matter. A trained advocate can talk it through with you without you having to label it first. You're allowed to just say "something happened and I'm not okay."

What you do next is yours

Reaching out for support does not commit you to reporting anything, telling anyone, or taking any particular step. Many people talk to a hotline first precisely to understand their options. Doing nothing yet is also a valid choice. The point is that you don't have to carry it alone while you decide.

This page won't ask you to relive it

There's no form here asking what happened, and nothing you do on this page is recorded. The people on the other end of these lines are trained to hold that conversation with care — a webpage isn't the right place for it, and we won't pretend to be.

Who to reach

Any of these is a good first call. You can stay anonymous, and you can stop whenever you want.

RAINN — National Sexual Assault Hotline
800-656-4673
Free, confidential, 24/7, in English and Spanish. Call, or chat at rainn.org/hotline, or text HOPE to 64673. Trained specialists who can listen, help you understand options, and connect you to local services — whether something happened yesterday or years ago.
988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline24/7 · ANY CRISIS
Call or text 988 (US) for any emotional crisis — not only thoughts of suicide. Free and confidential.
National Domestic Violence Hotline24/7
1-800-799-7233, or text START to 88788. For anything involving a partner, ex, or someone in your home — including safety planning.
Cyber Civil Rights InitiativeIMAGE ABUSE
1-844-878-2274. If it involves photos or videos shared without consent, they're the specialists — and they handle the emotional side too.
If you're a student

Help that's closer than you think

Most colleges and universities have free, confidential resources built for exactly this — you don't have to be sure anything "official" happened to use them.

Your campus counseling centerCONFIDENTIAL
Free short-term counseling for enrolled students. Often the most confidential option on campus — search your school's name plus "counseling center."
Your Title IX officeRIGHTS & OPTIONS
Every US school that receives federal funding has one. They can explain your options and supportive measures — talking to them doesn't obligate you to file anything. Search your school's name plus "Title IX."
Campus victim advocateSUPPORT
Many schools have a confidential advocate whose only job is to support you and walk beside you, at your pace. Search your school's name plus "victim advocate" or "survivor advocate."
